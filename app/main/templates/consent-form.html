Background and Purpose of the Study
Due to the shortcomings of current artificial intelligence (AI) techniques and natural language understanding, conversational interfaces (also known as chatbots) are not yet capable of dealing with the complexity of conversational interactions, often resulting in breakdowns or failures during conversations.
Crowd-powered conversational systems (CPCS) have been proposed as a remedy to these shortcomings of AI. These CPCSs leverage human intelligence in real time to tackle range of conversational tasks, such as stress mitigation, information searching, among others.
In the CPCS, multiple humans (also known as crowd workers) are involved in generating chatbot's responses in real time (see Figure 1). Then either computational intelligence or human evaluators are employed to choose the best quality response (message 2 from the Figure 1) in real time. Hence both processes—response generation and response evaluation—work in parallel. Nevertheless, at times, a response can also be generated directly through computational intelligence if it has enough knowledge of the topic.
We have implemented CPCS in the form of a chatbot for this study.
The purpose of the research is to investigate your perception of the chatbot, which is augmented by “crowd-powered support in real time”.
Caution: You are going to interact with a chatbot which is powered by "real humans". Please do not use cursing, abusive, racial, or culturally insensitive language while conversing with a crowd-powered chatbot.
